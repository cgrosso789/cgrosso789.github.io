I 💘 Boston

Boston University was an amazing place at which to "retool."

I began my "come-back journey" in January of 2018 with C++ classes at Nashua Community College,

and it was there that a professor suggested I ought to go to grad school at BU. The next day, after the shock of that novel idea had worn off, I concluded he was absolutely right!

My professor had originally suggested I study CS. But while checking the BU course catalog, I noticed that they had something called an MSSD. Software development was REALLY what I wanted to do, and I had never heard of an MSSD before. Eight courses and no thesis. How tough could that be?

Right. I'd missed out on quite a few years of IT, so I had to scramble to keep up, and considering the intensity of the learning, most nights I'd burn the 3 a.m. oil. As I've told my kids, "Nothing worthwhile ever comes cheap." Graduate school at Boston University has been the embodiment of that expression, and I am extremely grateful to have had the opportunity to study here. Below I've listed the courses I've taken and the term project that I did for each. Where able, I've included a link to further information about that project. I am most proud of the Quilt Fabric Estimator project; I loved the Design Patterns course.
