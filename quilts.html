It was in Copley Place in Boston that I first fell in love...

I don't remember what I was doing in the city that particular day in 1983 or 84,

but it caught my eye from within a display window inside the elegant, high-end mall. It was blue and white, and all crinkly and soft-looking. It was made of triangles and squares, and seemed as though someone's great-grandmother may have lovingly stitched it together by a fireplace, and that it had since seen both joys and hardships of years past along with the family that had taken nightly refuge under it. It was stunning. And I knew right then that I had to learn to quilt.

The first quilt book I ever owned I purchased a few years later in a used book store in Bar Harbor, Maine, entitled, not surprisinly, Folk Quilts and How to Recreate Them, by Audrey & Douglas Wiss. It was pretty-much instruction-free, but contained within its wonderous pages beautiful pictures of antique quilts. It was not actually until I had had my first child and stayed at home that I was able to pursue my new passion, and discovered, to my glee, that there existed quilting guilds and shops aplenty in the world! The guilds existed for people like me, and to keep quilting alive; the shops existed for us to happily throw whatever money they wanted at them in exchange for an artist's pallet of wonderful fabrics.

Since those early days, I have made probably 100 or more quilts of various sizes and--how shall we say it--"loveliness levels." I rarely photographed them, and most of them were given away as gifts. I find that, mostly, I do not lament my lack of pictorial records of my endeavors, because the endeavors were never about my glory, but about sharing what I considered special and precious with someone whom I wanted to make feel supremely loved. However, now that I am making this website, more pictures of the good, the bad and even the ugly would have been really nice!! Come and take a peek at a few things anyway!

Some Show 'n' Tell:

For many years, I tried to keep up with all the new babies at church, having determined that each should have a handmade quilt from me! I made good on my promise to myself for probably five years, until conflicting passions of mine (namely, our first show dog, and two years later, the adoption of our precious daughter from China) caused me to tuck away quilting for about nine years.

All quilts on this page were pieced by Tini Grosso; some were machine-quilted by Jessie Sweeney of Coda Quilting, Nashua, NH.
