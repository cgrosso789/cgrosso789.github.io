my "park"

I don't know what it is about wild (or even tame) animals that sooooo fascinates me...

but it does.

I can't wait to take my dog Wilson for a walk in the woods everyday just for the chance to catch some critter unawares (even though having Wilson in tow makes that less likely)! I've managed to take pictures of quite a few of them, but there are probably a hundred more, from deer to muskrats to spotted newts (and even a Bald Eagle, once!) of which I have no photo record. But they all hang out here--or at least temporarily visit--in my yard!!!! I have to pinch myself sometimes 😊! In any case, I've included many here for you to enjoy. (All except two of these photos actually come from my yard, but since I have seen the animals represented in those photos here, as well, I decided to include them anyway.)

I have to say that this obsession of mine with animals started a long time ago. My parents were too practical to want pets; but they had me, and thus had to endure the begging for YEARS until they could take it no longer and relented. I had a rabbit at first. I took THAT for walks, too; no leash--it followed me wherever I went. Then, my brother and I had squirrels--seriously! (See the front page article entitled, "Rescued." The photo is junk, but the "Christine" person is me.) Then I finally got a dog: a belligerent but very intelligent Alaskan Malamute that I loved with all my heart, and which put my dog education into overdrive. Fast-forward to marriage, kids...and more dogs. First a sweet Rottweiler that my friends called "The LickWeiler." Then a Greater Swiss Mountain Dog that I owner-handled to a champion title and bred. You can see her picture on this site--her registered name was Sunhaven's Miss Moneypenny. (Her championship was acknowleged at the GSMDCA National specialty, here, under Owner-Handled Champions.) When I walked THIS dog, our cat would often come with us, as well as a handful of chickens; no lie. (My brother should have nicknamed me "Snow White.")

But I seriously digress. We now have a lovable lab/coonhound mix named "Wilson" who adores all human beings, chases airplanes and accompanies me on critter-walks. Would you like to come along? Scroll down, and feel free to exit if you get bored; however, I hope you make it to the bottom of the page and are able to view the movie of the beaver!!
